White Water Rafting FAQ

What is white water rafting?

White water rafting is an outdoor recreational activity that uses an inflatable raft to navigate through rough water, typically in a river or other bodies of water with varying degrees of rough water. It is often done in groups with a guide for safety.

Is white water rafting safe?

While white water rafting involves navigating through challenging waters, it is generally safe when proper safety measures are followed. Always choose a reputable rafting company, wear appropriate safety gear, and follow the guide's instructions.

What should I wear for white water rafting?

It's recommended to wear comfortable, quick-drying clothing. A swimsuit, shorts, and a rash guard are common choices. You should also wear secure footwear, such as water shoes, and don't forget to bring a change of clothes for after the rafting trip.

Do I need prior experience to go white water rafting?

No, most white water rafting trips are suitable for beginners. Rafting companies often provide training and safety instructions before the trip. However, it's essential to choose a rafting route that matches your skill level.
